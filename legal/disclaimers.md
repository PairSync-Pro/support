## 3. Hypothetical and Backtested Performance

### 3.1 Backtests Are Hypothetical
**ALL BACKTESTED PERFORMANCE RESULTS SHOWN IN PAIRSYNC ARE HYPOTHETICAL.**

This means:
- They are based on historical data analysis applied retroactively
- They assume perfect execution at closing prices with no delays
- They do NOT represent actual trading results
- They were generated AFTER the fact (not in real-time)

### 3.2 In-Sample Optimization
**CRITICAL LIMITATION:**

PairSync's backtests are based on **in-sample optimization**, which means:
- The PairNet MLOps process was trained on the same historical data used for backtesting
- Entry/exit distances, timing parameters, and other settings were optimized to maximize performance on past data
- This creates **survivorship bias** and **overfitting risk**
- Out-of-sample performance (real-world future trading) is typically **20-40% lower** than in-sample backtests

**This is a standard limitation of quantitative strategies, but users MUST understand it.**

### 3.3 Not Indicative of Future Results
**PAST PERFORMANCE DOES NOT GUARANTEE FUTURE RESULTS.**

Even if a pair historically showed:
- High Sharpe ratios
- Consistent profitability
- Low drawdowns
- Strong cointegration

**There is NO guarantee:**
- The pattern will continue
- Future trades will be profitable
- Cointegration will persist
- The strategy will work going forward

**Market conditions change. Historical relationships break down. Strategies stop working.**

### 3.4 Backtests Exclude Real-World Costs
PairSync backtests assume **perfect execution** and do NOT include:

**Transaction Costs:**
- Brokerage commissions (typically $0.50-$5 per trade)
- SEC fees and regulatory charges
- Exchange fees

**Market Impact:**
- **Slippage:** Difference between expected and actual execution price
- **Spread costs:** Bid-ask spreads (can be 0.1%-1% per trade)
- **Market impact:** Your orders moving prices (especially for larger positions)

**Timing Issues:**
- Backtests assume execution at the exact closing price
- Real trading happens during market hours with intraday volatility
- Delays between signal generation and order placement

**Borrowing Costs:**
- **Short-selling fees:** Hard-to-borrow stock fees (can be 5%-50% annual or more)
- **Margin interest:** Cost of borrowing to leverage positions

**REALISTIC EXPECTATION:**  
If a backtest shows a 12% annual return, real-world trading might yield **8-10%** after costs (or less).

### 3.5 Survivorship Bias
PairSync analyzes pairs that currently exist in our universe. We do NOT account for:
- **Delisted stocks** (bankruptcies, acquisitions, delistings)
- **Pairs that broke down** and were removed from consideration
- **Structural breaks** that ended cointegration

This creates **survivorship bias** - we're showing you the "winners" that survived, not the pairs that failed.

## 6. Market Risks

### 6.1 General Market Risk
**ALL SECURITIES TRADING INVOLVES RISK OF LOSS.**

Even with pairs trading and market-neutral strategies:
- **Both legs can lose money simultaneously** (correlation breakdown)
- **Markets can remain irrational** longer than you can stay solvent
- **Black swan events** can destroy positions (2008 crisis, COVID-19, etc.)
- **Liquidity can evaporate** when you need to exit

### 6.2 Pairs Trading Specific Risks

**Cointegration Breakdown:**
- Two historically cointegrated stocks can **permanently diverge**
- Reasons: merger/acquisition, business model change, sector rotation
- Risk: Waiting for mean reversion that never comes

**Correlation Risk:**
- Even cointegrated pairs can temporarily move together in the same direction
- In market crashes, correlations often spike to 1.0 (everything falls together)
- Risk: Both legs lose money simultaneously

**Timing Risk:**
- Mean reversion may take longer than expected
- Your capital may be tied up for weeks or months
- Opportunity cost of waiting

**Regime Change Risk:**
- Market regimes shift (low vol → high vol, bull → bear)
- Strategies that worked in one regime fail in another
- 2020s market structure is different from in-sample training period

### 6.3 Leverage and Margin Risk
Pairs trading is often done with leverage (margin):
- **Amplified losses:** 2x leverage means 2x losses
- **Margin calls:** Broker can force liquidation if losses exceed margin
- **Interest costs:** Borrowing costs eat into returns
- **Forced exit:** May be closed out at worst possible time

**Never use more leverage than you can afford to lose.**

### 6.4 Short Selling Risk
Pairs trading involves **short selling** (betting against stocks):

**Risks:**
- **Unlimited loss potential:** Stocks can rise indefinitely (unlike long positions which max out at -100%)
- **Hard-to-borrow fees:** Some stocks are expensive to borrow (5%-100%+ annual)
- **Short squeezes:** Rapid price spikes force shorts to cover at losses
- **Dividend risk:** You pay dividends on shorted stocks
- **Recall risk:** Broker can force you to close short positions without notice

### 6.5 Liquidity Risk
- **Illiquid pairs:** Small-cap or low-volume stocks may have wide bid-ask spreads
- **Unable to exit:** Can't close position at desired price
- **Slippage:** Large orders move prices against you
